# Measles-SIR-Models
## Final Course Project for Special Topics in Biostatistics

# Abstract
Measles, Mumps, and Rubella (MMR), more commonly known as Measles, is a highly contagious, vaccine-preventable infection characterized by a high fever, cough, and runny nose. It is spread through respiratory droplets and airborne particles. Currently, the US is experiencing a potential outbreak of measles due to lowered MMR vaccination rates, with only 92.5% immunity reached in the last 5 years. This project uses a SIR model to compare the spread of measles infections and potential outbreaks between different vaccination rates (80\%, 85\%, 90\%, 95\%) and baseline reproduction rates (12 or 18) using simulated US data. Comparing SIR models showed that compared to the herd immunity benchmark of 95% vaccination coverage, reductions in vaccination rates resulted in substantial increases in total infections.
